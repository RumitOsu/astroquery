# Historic and Current Space Missions

## The Space Race Era (1957-1975)

### Sputnik and Early Milestones
The Space Age began on October 4, 1957, when the Soviet Union launched Sputnik 1, the first artificial satellite. This 83.6 kg polished metal sphere orbited Earth every 96 minutes, transmitting radio signals for 21 days. The following month, Sputnik 2 carried Laika, a stray dog from Moscow, into orbit — the first living creature in space (she did not survive re-entry). The United States responded with Explorer 1 in January 1958, which discovered the Van Allen radiation belts.

### Yuri Gagarin and Human Spaceflight
On April 12, 1961, Soviet cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin became the first human in space aboard Vostok 1, completing a single orbit of Earth in 108 minutes. The US followed with Alan Shepard's suborbital flight on May 5, 1961, and John Glenn's orbital flight on February 20, 1962. President John F. Kennedy's 1961 speech to Congress set the goal of landing a man on the Moon before the end of the decade.

### Apollo Program
NASA's Apollo program achieved the first crewed Moon landing on July 20, 1969, when Apollo 11 astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walked on the lunar surface while Michael Collins orbited above. Armstrong's first words on the Moon — "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind" — became one of the most famous quotes in history. Six Apollo missions successfully landed on the Moon (Apollo 11, 12, 14, 15, 16, and 17), with Apollo 13 famously surviving an oxygen tank explosion to return safely. Apollo 17 in December 1972 was the last crewed mission to the Moon. The Apollo program returned 382 kg of lunar samples.

## Space Stations

### Mir
The Soviet/Russian space station Mir operated from 1986 to 2001 and was the first modular space station. It hosted astronauts from multiple nations and demonstrated long-duration human spaceflight. Cosmonaut Valeri Polyakov spent 437.7 consecutive days aboard Mir (1994-1995), a record for continuous time in space.

### International Space Station (ISS)
The ISS is the largest structure humans have built in space, measuring 109 meters across — roughly the size of a football field. It orbits at about 408 km altitude, traveling at 27,600 km/h and completing an orbit every 90 minutes. Construction began in 1998, and it has been continuously inhabited since November 2, 2000. The ISS is a collaboration between NASA, Roscosmos, ESA, JAXA, and CSA. Over 270 individuals from 21 countries have visited the station. The ISS has hosted thousands of scientific experiments in microgravity covering biology, physics, materials science, and Earth observation.

## Planetary Exploration

### Mars Exploration
Mars has been the most explored planet beyond Earth. Key missions include:
- **Viking 1 & 2 (1976)**: First successful Mars landers, conducted soil experiments to search for life
- **Mars Pathfinder (1997)**: Deployed the first Mars rover, Sojourner
- **Spirit & Opportunity (2004)**: Twin rovers; Opportunity operated for over 14 years and traveled 45.16 km
- **Curiosity (2012-present)**: Car-sized rover in Gale Crater, discovered organic molecules and ancient lake environments
- **Perseverance (2021-present)**: Exploring Jezero Crater, collecting samples for future return to Earth, carries the Ingenuity helicopter (first powered flight on another planet)
- **Ingenuity (2021-2024)**: The Mars helicopter completed 72 flights, far exceeding its planned 5-flight technology demonstration

### Venus Missions
- **Venera Program (1961-1984)**: Soviet missions to Venus; Venera 7 (1970) was the first spacecraft to transmit data from another planet's surface; Venera 13 (1982) survived 127 minutes on Venus's surface and sent back color photographs
- **Magellan (1990-1994)**: Mapped 98% of Venus's surface using synthetic aperture radar
- **VERITAS and DAVINCI**: NASA missions planned for the late 2020s to study Venus's surface and atmosphere

### Outer Planet Missions
- **Pioneer 10 & 11 (1972-1973)**: First spacecraft to traverse the asteroid belt and fly by Jupiter and Saturn
- **Voyager 1 & 2 (1977)**: Grand Tour of the outer planets. Voyager 2 is the only spacecraft to have visited all four giant planets. Voyager 1 is the most distant human-made object at over 160 AU from the Sun. Both carry Golden Records with sounds and images of Earth.
- **Galileo (1995-2003)**: Orbited Jupiter for 8 years, dropped a probe into Jupiter's atmosphere, discovered evidence of subsurface oceans on Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto
- **Cassini-Huygens (2004-2017)**: Orbited Saturn for 13 years. The Huygens probe landed on Titan in January 2005, the most distant landing from Earth ever achieved. Discovered Enceladus's ocean and geysers.
- **Juno (2016-present)**: Orbiting Jupiter in polar orbits, studying its atmosphere, magnetic field, interior structure, and auroras
- **New Horizons (2015)**: Flew by Pluto, revealing mountains, glaciers, and a thin atmosphere. Later flew by Kuiper Belt Object Arrokoth in 2019.

## Space Telescopes

### Hubble Space Telescope
Launched in April 1990, Hubble orbits at 547 km altitude and has made over 1.5 million observations. Despite an initially flawed mirror corrected by the 1993 servicing mission, Hubble has transformed our understanding of the universe — from determining the age of the universe to discovering that its expansion is accelerating (leading to the 2011 Nobel Prize in Physics). Hubble observes in ultraviolet, visible, and near-infrared wavelengths.

### James Webb Space Telescope
JWST launched on December 25, 2021, and orbits at the L2 Lagrange point, 1.5 million km from Earth. Its 6.5-meter gold-coated primary mirror (composed of 18 hexagonal segments) is the largest ever launched into space. Operating primarily in infrared, JWST can see further back in time than any previous telescope, observing galaxies that formed within the first few hundred million years after the Big Bang. It has revolutionized exoplanet atmospheric science, stellar formation studies, and our understanding of the early universe.

## Upcoming Missions
- **Artemis Program**: NASA's program to return humans to the Moon, with Artemis II planned as the first crewed flight around the Moon since 1972
- **Europa Clipper (2024)**: NASA mission to study Jupiter's moon Europa, focusing on its subsurface ocean and habitability
- **Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope**: NASA's next great observatory, designed for wide-field infrared surveys, dark energy research, and exoplanet detection via microlensing
- **LISA (Laser Interferometer Space Antenna)**: ESA-led mission to detect gravitational waves from space, planned for the 2030s
